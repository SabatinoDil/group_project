Black Bears

The most common of the bears from North America is the black bear or american bear, this specie is found in 40 of the 50 states in the US. The black bear is listed by the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) as status of Least Concern because his population number is increasing. The black bear is the smallest and most widely distributed bear of all the species in the continent. They have a diet that depends of the season of the year and location. Because this specie is omnivore they can be found in forested areas in undisturbed habitats and rural regions, although this specie can easily adapt to surviving in hybrid landscapes of fragmented urban and rural characteristics were food is easily accessible with presence of vegetative coverage. In general bears have intelligence comparable to that of the great apes and are highly evolved social animals. They also has better sense of hearing and eyesight compared to humans, also their sense of smell is the keenest of all them, it's seven times greater than a dog's. They are strong swimmers doing that for pleasure and to feed. Another characteristic is that they communicate with various vocal and non-vocal sounds. At the age of 3 to 5 years black bears usually produce their first litter, and their breeding period takes from June to August. They are promiscuous and his breeding period lasts for 2 to 3 months. Average lifespan of black bears in the wild is 18 years while in captivity was 44 years.Another characteristics that made bears special animals is their capacity to hibernate, black bears typically hibernate between 3 to 8 months, depending of regional climate.
